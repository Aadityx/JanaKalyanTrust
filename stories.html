💧 Clean Water Initiative

Turning Challenges into Change: Veer Tembi Wadi's Water Story

Veer Tembi Wadi is a small tribal hamlet comprising 35 families with a population of approximately 125 people. Located about 100 meters from Veer Railway Station and close to the highway, the villagers primarily depend on daily wage labour for their livelihood. Employment opportunities are irregular, forcing many families to migrate seasonally in search of work. Some engage in brick-making, while others take up any available labour work to support their families. During periods of migration, entire families often relocate, affecting their stability and quality of life.

One of the major challenges faced by the community was the lack of a reliable water supply. Many families did not own agricultural land, and access to water was extremely limited. Women and children had to travel long distances every day to fetch water for their household needs, making daily life difficult and time-consuming.

Recognising these challenges, Jan Kalyan Trust, along with Centre for Social Action (CSA), organised a village meeting (Gavkhi) to understand the concerns of the community and explore possible solutions. During the discussions, villagers openly shared their difficulties and identified water scarcity as one of their most pressing issues.

The community pointed out that there was a pond located on privately owned land, which was occasionally used by villagers for water collection. Through dialogue and collective decision-making, the villagers, along with representatives of the Gram Panchayat, approached the landowner and requested permission to develop the water source for the benefit of the entire community. Appreciating the needs of the village, the landowner agreed to support the initiative.

"Today, every household in Veer Tembi Wadi has access to water through a tap connection. What was once a daily struggle has been transformed into a sustainable community resource."

With the cooperation of the villagers, the Gram Panchayat, and the guidance of Jan Kalyan Trust, the pond was strengthened with a protective boundary structure and developed into a reliable water source. A water pump was installed, and a distribution system was established to ensure regular access to water. Families no longer spend hours fetching water, and the village now enjoys improved access to one of life's most essential necessities.

This transformation stands as a powerful example of how community participation, local leadership, and collective action can bring lasting change. Through facilitation, awareness, and collaboration, Jan Kalyan Trust helped the people of Veer Tembi Wadi turn a long-standing challenge into a story of empowerment and hope.

📚 Education & Hope

Reviving a Learning Space for the Children of Shirgav Wadi

Education has always been at the heart of Jan Kalyan Trust's mission to empower rural and tribal communities. Several years ago, the Trust established a small educational structure in Shirgav Wadi to support the learning needs of local children. Over time, however, the building deteriorated due to age and lack of maintenance, eventually becoming unsuitable for educational activities.

Recognising the need for a safe and functional learning environment, the social workers of Jan Kalyan Trust initiated discussions with the villagers about restoring the facility. They shared their vision of reviving the structure so that it could once again serve the educational needs of the community's children.

The proposal received a positive response from the villagers, who recognised the importance of having a dedicated learning space within the village. With the community's support, the matter was presented to the Gram Panchayat and subsequently discussed in the Gram Sabha. After careful consideration, the proposal was approved, paving the way for the renovation of the building.

"What was once a neglected structure has become a symbol of community participation and collective responsibility toward education."

Through the combined efforts of the villagers, local authorities, and Jan Kalyan Trust, the old structure was restored and transformed into a welcoming educational space. The building was repaired, strengthened, and freshly painted both inside and outside, creating an environment that is safe, attractive, and conducive to learning.

Today, the renovated centre serves approximately 22 children, providing them with a space where they can learn, grow, and build a brighter future. This success story demonstrates how collaboration between local communities, village institutions, and social action initiatives can revitalise existing resources and create meaningful opportunities for children. The transformation of the Shirgav Wadi learning centre stands as a testament to the power of community-driven development and the belief that every child deserves access to a supportive learning environment.
