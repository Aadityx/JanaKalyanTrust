Our Farm

Agriculture at the Heart of Our Mission

Agriculture plays a vital role in the mission of Jankalyan Trust. Through a variety of farming and allied agricultural activities, the Trust promotes sustainable livelihoods, nutritional security, and self-reliance.

Our agricultural initiatives not only support the needs of the boarding home but also serve as practical models of responsible farming and resource management. From dairy and turmeric to paddy, vegetables, and fruits — every crop grown here nurtures a future.

Our Agricultural Initiatives

What We Grow & Raise

Integrated farming divisions that sustain the boarding home and empower the community.

Dairy Unit

Our dairy unit provides fresh milk for the boarding children and produces pure, traditional ghee. Surplus dairy products are marketed locally, generating income while offering the community high-quality nutrition. This integrated approach promotes wholesome nutrition, sustainable resource utilization, and economic self-reliance.

Turmeric Cultivation

A key initiative covering the entire value chain — from planting and cultivation to harvesting, processing, powder preparation, and marketing. By maintaining high quality standards throughout, the Trust provides consumers with pure, natural turmeric that offers both quality and value for money.

Paddy Cultivation

Paddy farming contributes significantly to the food requirements of the boarding home. The rice produced is primarily used for the meals of the boarder children, ensuring a reliable source of nutritious food. The paddy straw and hay are utilized as cattle feed, creating an efficient cycle of resource utilization.

Vegetable Cultivation

A variety of seasonal vegetables are cultivated, including cucumber, snake gourd, bitter gourd, pumpkins, and other locally suitable crops. These vegetables provide fresh and healthy produce for the market while promoting sustainable farming practices and nutritional well-being.

Fruit Cultivation

A diverse range of fruits is cultivated on the farmland, including watermelon, strawberries, mangoes, cashew, jackfruit, and other seasonal fruits — contributing to nutritional diversity and sustainable agricultural practice on the farm.

Animal Husbandry

In addition to dairy, our animal husbandry program focuses on raising goats (goatry) and poultry. This supports tribal livelihoods, provides secondary income sources for women, and contributes organic manure for our crop cultivation.
